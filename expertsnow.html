Project brief

  • Client

Fictitious project given by CareerFoundry as part of my UX Design training.

  • Context

"We all need the advice of an expert sometimes, and how-to videos don’t always cut it. The goal of this app is to give people a simple, intuitive way to connect with an expert in nearly any field within seconds so they can feel more informed and more prepared to face their everyday (and not-so-everyday) problems."

  • Objective

Create a web responsive app enabling users to reach experts of any professional fields whenever and wherever they need.

DISCOVER

To better understand the potential users’ expectations, I analyzed some of ExpertsNow competitors and interviewed 4 potential users.

Competitive analysis
Who are the other actors on the market?

Doing a competitive analysis made me understand the market better. Also, I was able to determine what ExpertsNow could bring to the table.

I carried out a SWOT analysis with 2 competitors : Doctolib and Pages Jaunes.

User's interviews
Who are the potential users? What are their expectations?

With the results from the competitive analysis in mind, I prepared and underwent interviews with potential users in order to empathize with them and better understand their needs.

I analysed the results through affinity maps to have a better overview of the information received.

DEFINE

The results from the Discover phase were very useful in defining types of user who would be using ExpertsNow, potential solutions to answer their needs and a navigation flow that would facilitate their experience.

Personas
What are the different types of user?

To better empathize with users throughout the design process, I created 3 personas. Each defining a type of user.

For instance, Glorieuse represents the users of 50+ years old who would need the app to be simple to use and safe regarding payment.

Solutions to user's needs
What are the current pain points the users have? What could be the solutions?

With my 3 user personas, I was able to define difficulties that users are currently experiencing. And so, think about potential solutions to solve those pain points.

Problem #1: Getting directly in touch with an expert from any given professional field for advice is laborious.

Solution #1: An app that connects private individual directly with experts and without intermediary.

Problem #2: Looking for the right expert online can be confusing and time consuming.

Solution #2: An app that gives access to a broad verified experts list to contact at anytime.

User's flows
How would the users be navigating through the app?

Following the three previous potential solutions, I created 3 user flows. For each, I focused on one persona’s needs.

User flow after Usability Test & Refining the design

DEVELOP

With the previous user flows in mind, I was able to map out ExpertsNow and start designing its different screens.

Information Architecture
How is the app organised?

Thanks to my personas’ user flows, I was able to map out ExpertsNow. This sitemap gave me an overview of how the app should be organized and all the different screens needed.

Sitemap after Usability Test & Refining the design

Wireframing
What is the design of the different screens?

With the sitemap in mind, I was able to start designing the different screens from low-fidelity to mid-fidelity.

I made modifications on the wireframes along the way from low-fi to mid-fi, according to feedback I was receiving from my CareerFoundry tutor.

Prototyping
Time to turn the screens into an interactive prototype in preparation for testing.

Once I finished creating the mid-fi wireframes, I linked them into an interactive prototype via MarvelApp.

Flow #1: How to sign up onto ExpertsNow

Flow #2: How to put an expert into one's favorites

Flow #3: How to book an appointment with an expert

Thanks to this prototype, it was easier to experience how the navigation through the app would feel.

DELIVER

With the interactive prototype and some mid-fi wireframes, I performed a Usability and a Preference test to check if the organization and navigation flow of ExpertsNow were intuitive enough.

Usability Test
Is the app working well? Is is straightforward enough?

I performed moderated usability tests to assess the intuitiveness of ExpertsNow (1 in-person & 5 remote).

To well grasp all the pain points from the test participants, I analyzed their answers via affinity maps and then, through a rainbow spreadsheet.

In my design iteration process, I focused on the 5 most urgent issues. Below is one of the modification I made according to feedback received during the Usability test.

Example: modification of the sign-up page

#Error: Lack of a straightforward way to go to the Log in page from the Sign up page.

#Evidence: During the test, all participants felt confused when asked to get from the sign up page to the log in page.

#Iteration: At the bottom of the page, I added a question and hyperlink to facilitate the transition to the Log in page in case of mistake.

Preference Tests
Which wording and layouts make the users less confused?

Participants were mainly confused while on the Homepage, Explore page and Booking page.

So, I carried out 3 preference tests to come up with the best solutions to avoid further confusion.

REFINE

After testing my prototype and implementing the different tests participants’ feedback, I focused on the visual aspect of ExpertsNow by creating hi-fi wireframes.

Reflexions on UI Design
How does the visual design should look like?

For the UI Design of ExpertsNow, I had to come up with my own color strategy. I then refined the design of the app according to Gestalt Laws, the Principle of Design and Material guidelines.

Style Guide
Which colors, which fonts, which icons, etc?

After determining a color strategy and while following different design principles and guidelines, I created a UI kit for ExpertsNow to facilitate the new iteration process but also to prepare for a possible handover with developers.

I documented this UI kit in a Style Guide (includes updates from Collaborative Design feedback & from following Web Content Accessibility Guidelines).

ExpertsNow Style Guide
Final Design
How does the app look like after implementing feedback and adding visual design?

In a second Iteration Process, I first asked 3 of my fellow CareerFoundry students to review ExpertsNow in order to give me feedback from UX Designers point of view.

Example of modification after Collaborative Design: the Explore page

Modification #1: My peers agreed the white text from the “Search by Experts” section to not be readable enough.

Iteration #1: To lessen this pain point, I decided to redesign the page and put the text underneath a picture for more readability. I kept the idea of having pictures since it is considered more welcoming, and I added the top message to further the wanted “warm feeling” of the app.

Modification #2: The “How it works” section was considered too repetitive and not relevant to be kept after registration/connection.

Iteration #2: I removed it and fully focused on emphasizing the two type of research since it is the Explore page after all.

Then, I took another look at the app to improve its accessibility according to Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG).

Example of modification after Accessibility Design: the Log-in page

#Objective: Meeting the Success Criterion 2.5.3: Label in name of the WCAG in order to "ensure that people with disabilities can rely on visible labels as a means to interact with the components".

#Iteration: To improve the readability of form labels, ExpertsNow will display permanent and out-of-the-field labels.

Finally, I updated ExpertsNow prototype after having implementing the last feedback I received.

CONCLUSION

After the second iteration process, I finalized updating my prototype. I then reflected on what could the next steps be to further improve the app and what outcomes could I take from this training project.

What could be the next steps?
  1. Make new improvements according to the other 14 pain points that the participants pointed out.
  2. Carry out new Preference tests to get significant enough results in order to implement the best solutions possible.
  3. Verify if the app is indeed « accessible », I should carry out a Usability Test with relevant potential users.
What do I keep from this project?

During this project I learnt in details what the UX Design Process was. While working on some of the different and varied steps I went through, I felt frustrated at times because of my lack of experience. But I know now how to improve and be more efficient regarding certain steps (especially Usability Test preparation, Hi-Fi wireframing and UI design). I also learnt how to use different tools, such as Adobe XD and it was really fun. Finally, what I will remember from this project is how much I enjoyed it. Creating something according to users’ needs & wants felt so satisfying and fulfilling. I could give sense to what I was doing and I knew that all the modifications I was bringing to ExpertsNow were in the best interest of potential future users.
